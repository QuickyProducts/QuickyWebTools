Quicky
MUSIC

Full-length songs in Second Life, played gapless from short clips.

Put music on it

  1. Cut your songs into clips with the Music Slicer tool (opens in any browser, no install). Drop in a song, trim off any junk, hit Export.
  2. Unzip the download. The .wav clips are right inside, already named correctly.
  3. In Second Life, bulk-upload the .wav files of a song (Build, Upload, Bulk).
  4. Drag all the uploaded clips into the player's Contents. It registers them by itself, tidies up, and the song appears in the list. Repeat for more songs. Long songs: viewers sometimes move only part of a big batch per drag - if the player says "waiting", just drag the rest in and it registers once the set is complete.
Clips are stored inside the player and delete themselves from its Contents once they are safely stored. The kB readout shows how much of the 127 kB storage is in use - long songs take more of it.

The menu (touch it)

The dialog shows what just played, what plays now (with the song length) and what comes next - an ∞ in front of a title means repeat is active. The footer line shows volume, song count and free storage.

► PlayStart shuffle play. Every song plays once, then the player stops - unless repeat is on. While music is playing the same button shows ■ Stop.
» SkipJump to the next song.
✘ CloseClose your menu session so the next person can use the player right away.
♪ VolumeOpens the volume menu: one button per level, 10% to 100%.
∞ RepeatCycles the repeat mode: current song → all songs → off. The ∞ marks in the menu show which one is active.
≡ ListShow all songs in chat. Click a title to play it. Each line also has ✚ (queue it as the next song), ✘ (delete it, asks first) and ⇪ (export just that song).
⌕ SearchType part of a title - one match plays right away, several come back as a clickable list, none and the search box reopens.
⚡ ExtrasOwner only. Holds the module buttons: Anims (chapter below), FX - glowing notes drifting off the speaker while music plays (Pink, Blue or Mix), Export (chapter below), and Swing on the dangling speaker. Only installed modules show up.
⌂ AccessOwner only. Who may use the menu: Owner, Group or Public.
With Group or Public access, whoever touches the player gets the menu for 60 seconds (every click extends it). Others see who is using it; only the owner can take over early. Guests get the playback controls but cannot delete, export or open Extras.

Export (Extras)

The ⇪ on a list line exports that song. In Extras → Export you choose how:

LinkChat shows the song title plus links to a temporary web page with the notecard name and content - copy from there.
ChatThe full notecard content arrives as chat blocks (25 clips each) - paste them in order into one notecard.
The result is a Mewsic/Nyanko-style notecard: first line like 30:21, then one clip UUID per line. Any QuickyMusic player imports it automatically when you drop the notecard in.

Animations (optional)

Drop any animations into the player's Contents, then touch and open ⚡ Extras, Anims:

HoldPlays the whole time you wear the player (a hold pose). Opens a picker with the animations inside - pick one, or Clear to remove it.
PlayPlays only while music is playing (a performance). Same picker.
With exactly one animation inside, it plays automatically - no setup at all (the Anims button then offers ⚠ Activate to unlock the two-slot mode for experienced users). If sitting down, standing up, a teleport or revoked permissions interrupt an animation, it restarts by itself. Tip: upload poses with priority 4 or higher so furniture and AOs don't override them.

FAQ

The player says "parts 1-42/100 - waiting"? Some clips have not arrived yet - viewers do not always move a big batch in one drag. Drag the missing ones in again. Still stuck? Open the player's Contents and check the part numbers - every part from 01 to the total must be there. Re-drag whatever is missing and the song registers on its own.

I dropped clips and nothing happened? Give it a few seconds - the player waits shortly so a whole batch can arrive before it scans. If still nothing: the clips must keep their exact slicer names and must be full perm.

It says "needs full-perm clips - use notecard import instead"? Clips you received without full permissions cannot be registered directly. Ask the sender for an exported notecard instead - dropped into the player, it imports without needing any clips.

Others cannot use the menu? That is the Access setting (owner menu, ⌂ Access): Owner, Group or Public. And while one person uses the menu, it is reserved for them for 60 seconds - ✘ Close releases it early.

My menu stopped reacting? Your 60-second session ended. Just touch the player again for a fresh menu.

How much music fits in one player? The storage readout in the menu shows it (127 kB total). A three-minute song costs well under 1 kB, a full hour about 4.6 kB - so hundreds of normal songs.

How long can a song be? Songs up to about 8 hours are supported. The slicer switches to 3-digit part numbers past 99 clips - nothing else changes for you. Storage cost is roughly 4.6 kB per hour of song. Long songs need full-perm clips; without full perm, use the notecard import instead (drop an exported notecard into the player and it registers by itself).

How do I move my songs to another player? Export each song with the ⇪ on its List line, paste the result into a notecard named like the title, and drop that notecard into the other player. (Extras → Export only sets HOW the export arrives: web page link or chat blocks.)
