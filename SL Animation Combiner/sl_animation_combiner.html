What does this tool actually do?

An SL animation is a list of "joints" (skeleton bones) with keyframes per joint. This tool merges two files into one. The AUTO rules do what SL itself would do if you played both at once: for every bone that exists in both files, the one with the higher joint priority wins (tie → B). Duration and loop come from the longer file, the other file's motion cycle is tiled into it at original speed.

Pinned bones are dropped automatically: many animations carry static, do-nothing joints — e.g. an mPelvis frozen at zero that would kill your AO's stand sway. AUTO detects those (static and at rest pose) and sets them OFF. Open Joint Control to bring one back or to override any pick by hand: A, B, or OFF per bone or per group.

Cycle timing: bones taken from the file that does not drive the duration must be re-timed to fit the new length. Tile repeats that file's loop cycle at (almost) original speed until it fills the output loop — best for cyclic motion like typing or rubbing. Stretch squeezes the whole file once into the loop window — best for slow poses, but it slows the motion down. Raw copies key times 1:1 — only correct if both files have the same duration.

Joint priorities are kept per joint from their source file, so in-world layering keeps working the way it did with the two originals.
