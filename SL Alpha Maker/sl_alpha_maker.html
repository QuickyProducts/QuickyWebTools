How to use this in Second Life

1. (Optional) Load your own template — Click Load Upper… / Load Lower…, or drag&drop an image straight onto the canvas. The default templates (Linden Lab's official Fashion Design Templates) work for any mesh body that shares the classic SL UV layout. If your body ships its own template (Reborn, Legacy, Maitreya…) load it here so the wireframe matches exactly.

2. Pick a tool — BRUSH for free-hand. RECT / ELLIPSE drop a marquee that stays put after you release the mouse: drag inside it to reposition, switch between Rect and Ellipse to morph the shape, then hit the green ✓ (or Enter) to commit the cut — or the red ✗ (or Esc) to discard.

3. Erase / Restore — Drag with ERASE to make pixels transparent (checker pattern reveals where the body will be invisible). Switch to RESTORE (or hold Alt) to bring them back.

4. Undo — ↺ Undo (or Ctrl+Z) reverts the last action. Holds up to 30 steps in memory.

5. Download — Upper / Lower save individual PNGs. Both bundles them into a single alpha_mask.zip — one save dialog, two files inside. You get clean white-on-transparent PNGs with no decorative template artwork — just the alpha mask.

6. Upload to SL — Build → Upload → Image…, then apply via your body's HUD as a custom alpha layer. The transparent areas of the PNG become invisible body parts.

Default templates: Second Life™ Fashion Design Templates © 2003 Linden Research, Inc., CC BY 3.0 (flattened, upscaled to 1024×1024). Custom templates can be any image; they stay on your machine — nothing is uploaded.
